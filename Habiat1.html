The Jurassic Period

The Jurassic period, spanning from approximately 201 to 145 million years ago, was a time of major evolutionary development. It marked a significant increase in the diversity of dinosaurs, with the emergence of iconic species like the Brachiosaurus and Allosaurus.

This era saw the breakup of the supercontinent Pangaea, leading to the formation of the Atlantic Ocean and new continental arrangements. The climate was generally warm and humid, which supported lush vegetation and created diverse ecosystems.

During this period, herbivorous dinosaurs evolved to reach enormous sizes, while carnivores adapted to become more efficient hunters. The Jurassic also witnessed the rise of the first birds, which are believed to have evolved from theropod dinosaurs.

The end of the Jurassic period set the stage for the even greater diversification of dinosaur species in the subsequent Cretaceous period.
