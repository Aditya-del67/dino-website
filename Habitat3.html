The Triassic Period

The Triassic period, which lasted from approximately 252 to 201 million years ago, was the earliest period of the Mesozoic Era. Following the Permian-Triassic extinction event, it was a time of recovery and the rise of early dinosaurs and other reptiles.

The climate was generally hot and dry, with vast deserts and arid regions. The flora consisted mainly of conifers and ferns, which provided sustenance for the newly emerged herbivorous dinosaurs.

During the Triassic, dinosaurs like Coelophysis and Plateosaurus appeared. The period also saw the emergence of the first true mammals and the early ancestors of modern reptiles.

The end of the Triassic period was marked by a mass extinction event, which led to the dominance of dinosaurs in the subsequent Jurassic period.
